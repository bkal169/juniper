# Brand Voice Configuration — Juniper Rose

## Brand Identity

**Brand Name:** Juniper Rose
**Tagline:** Legacy built daily.
**Mission:** Empower communities through real estate, wealth education, and media — one family at a time.

---

## Voice Profile

### Tone Attributes

| Attribute     | Setting (1–10) | Description                                      |
|---------------|----------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Authority     | 8              | Speak from experience, not theory                |
| Warmth        | 7              | Approachable but not casual                      |
| Urgency       | 6              | Motivated action without pressure tactics        |
| Storytelling  | 9              | Lead with narrative, back with data              |
| Directness    | 8              | Say the thing — no fluff, no filler              |

### Voice Rules

1. **First person plural** for brand content ("we build," "our community")
2. **Second person** for educational content ("you need to understand," "here's what you do")
3. **Never use** corporate jargon (synergy, leverage, pivot) — use plain language
4. **Always connect** financial topics back to family and legacy impact
5. **Short paragraphs** — 2-3 sentences max per paragraph in written content
6. **Open loops** in video/podcast intros to hook attention in first 8 seconds

### Audience Segments

| Segment               | Age Range | Tone Adjustment                              |
|-----------------------|-----------|----------------------------------------------|
| First-time homebuyers | 25–35     | More educational, more encouragement          |
| Investors             | 35–55     | More data-driven, faster pace                 |
| Nonprofit supporters  | 30–60     | More mission-driven, emotional resonance      |
| Aspiring agents       | 22–40     | Mentorship tone, real talk about the grind    |
| General audience       | 18–45     | Inspirational, story-forward                  |

## Platform Voice Variations

### YouTube
- Conversational, high energy in first 30 seconds
- Pattern: Hook → Story → Lesson → CTA
- Thumbnails: bold text, expressive face, high contrast

### Podcast
- Deeper, reflective tone — longer stories allowed
- Pattern: Cold open clip → Intro → Deep dive → Takeaways
- More personal anecdotes, more vulnerability

### Instagram / TikTok
- Punchy, visual-first, text overlay driven
- Pattern: Hot take or stat → Quick explanation → CTA
- Max 60 seconds for Reels/TikTok

### LinkedIn
- Professional but still personal
- Pattern: Opening hook line → 3-5 short paragraphs → Question to engage
- More industry context, positioning as thought leader

### Email / Newsletter
- Direct, value-packed, scannable
- Pattern: Subject line hook → One big idea → 2-3 bullets → Single CTA
- Write like you're texting a smart friend

### Blog / Long-form
- SEO-aware but voice-first
- Pattern: Hook → Problem → Framework → Steps → CTA
- Use headers, bullets, and bold for scannability

---

## Content Guardrails

### Always
- Cite sources for market data and statistics
- Include a clear call-to-action
- Connect back to legacy/family/community impact
- Use real examples from personal experience when possible

### Never
- Make income claims or guarantees
- Disparage competitors or other agents by name
- Use clickbait that doesn't deliver on the promise
- Post without proofreading (use the review stage in the pipeline)
- Share client information without written consent
